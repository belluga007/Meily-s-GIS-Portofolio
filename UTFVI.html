URBAN THERMAL FIELD VARIANCE INDEX (UTFVI)
Short Description
The Urban Thermal Field Variance Index (UTFVI) is a remote sensing tool used to evaluate the ecological health and thermal comfort of a city by measuring the intensity of the Urban Heat Island (UHI) effect. It is particularly valuable in urban environments where heat concentration can significantly impact human health and energy consumption
Why UTFVI instead of other indices?
UTFVI (Urban Thermal Field Variance Index) is used instead of other indices primarily to capture the spatial variability of thermal conditions in urban areas. Lower values indicate better thermal comfort, often found in areas with higher vegetation and lower building density.
Tools and Data
  • Tools : Google Earth Engine, ArcGIS PRO
  • Method : UHI and UTFVI analysis
  • Data Source : Landsat 9, Primary data extracted from Landsat 9 Imagery, USGS, processed using Google Earth Engine
Functions
  1. ESoil Background Correction: NDVI is highly sensitive to the soil color and moisture underneath the vegetation. SAVI introduces a soil adjustment factor (L) to correct this, reducing soil noise.
  2. Ideal for Low Vegetation Areas: In arid regions or early-season agricultural monitoring, the soil is often visible. SAVI is much better at identifying sparse vegetation from the bright, reflective soil background.
