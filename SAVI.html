SOIL ADJUSTED VEGETATION INDEX (SAVI)
Short Description
The Soil-Adjusted Vegetation Index (SAVI) is a remote sensing tool used to estimate vegetation "greenness" while minimizing the misleading effects of soil brightness. It is an enchancement of the widely used NDVI and is particularly valuavle in arid or semi-arid regions where sparse plant cover leaves much of the soil exposed
Tools and Data
Tools : Google Earth Engine, ArcMap, Excel Method : SAVI = (NIR-red)/(NIR+red+L)x(1+L)
  1. This is script is the adaptation from https://github.com/ramiqcom/ebook-urban-gee.git By Ramadhan, Cited on August, 2024
  2. Sentinel-3 or Landsat, Primary data extracted from Landsat 9 Imagery, USGS, processed using Google Earth Engine
Functions
  1. Early-stage crop monitoring: assessing planting success before full canopies develop
  2. Dryland ecosystem tracking: monitoring rangelands or deserts where soil exposure is high
  3. Environmental restoration: tracking regrowth in post-fire areas ir reforestation zones
