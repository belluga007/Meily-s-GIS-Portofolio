Normalize Difference Moisture Index
Short Description
Tools
  • Google Earth Engine = processing Landsat imagery data to get NDMI
  • ArcMap = analyzing NDMI data and layouting

NDMI is a satellite-based index used to determine water content in vegetation and monitor water stress levels in plants. This index is particularly useful in precision agriculture for optimizing irrigation and detecting early signs of drought or waterlogging.
NDMI values ​​range from -1 to +1. Higher values ​​indicate healthy, well-watered vegetation, while lower values ​​indicate drought or water stress. The results of the NDMI analysis on the map show an uneven distribution pattern. Areas with bright hues (red-orange) indicate low humidity levels, and these results correspond to the distribution of settlements identified by utilizing the drastic differences in the spectral reflectance curves of these objects compared to vegetation.
Data
  • Primary data extracted from Landsat 9 Imagery, USGS, processed using Google Earth Engine
Functions
  1. Irrigation Management: Helps farmers plan irrigation schedules based on the actual needs of crops in a particular zone.
  2. Early Detection of Drought: Detects water stress earlier than visual indices such as NDVI because NDVI is sensitive to the internal water content of leaves.
  3. Fire Risk Analysis: Used to map fuel levels in areas prone to forest fires.
  4. Forest Monitoring: Detecting forest disturbances and changes in canopy health due to water shortages.
