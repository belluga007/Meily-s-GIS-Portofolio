Short Description
Tools : ArcMap
Method : Kernel Density, Equal Interval Classification
Heat map analysis is used to identify where features with either high or low values cluster spatially. The analysis works by looking at each features within the contact of neighboring features. Kernel Density method used to measure density of point pattern based on sample observations (buildings point) in this map. The result shows a visual representation of where the points are located and how they distributed in space. The data were classified using equal interval method. The analysis diagram shows that East Jakarta has the highest density class 1 and 2 areas, with density class classifications of (0 - 7.78) and (7.78 - 15.55) compared to other areas of Jakarta. Central Jakarta and North Jakarta are the two areas with the hottest points, class 5 with an area of ​​0.85 and 1.98 square kilometers, respectively. If observed, the centers of these two hot areas are located in the business and economic centers of Sudirman and Menteng (Central Jakarta) and Ancol (North Jakarta).
Data
  1. Shapefile Building points of Jakarta 2019, Indonesia Geospatial
  2. Indonesia Topographic Maps 2019, Indonesia Geospatial
Deficiencies and possible improvements
  1. The drawback of the data used is that it is only based on the location of building points without additional attributes such as population, so the analysis is only based on the number of points without measuring population density in the area.
  2. The classification method greatly influences the visualization of the smoothness of the distribution of hot and cold spots.
