LAND SURFACE TEMPERATURE (LST)
Short Description
Land Surface Temperature (LST) is a critical parameter in understanding the thermal properties of the Earth's surface. It is used in various applications such as urban heat island studies, agricultural monitoring, and climate research. LST can be derived from satellite imagery using thermal infrared sensors. LST is primarily controlled by surface albedo (reflectance), vegetation cover, and soil moisture. It is crucial for studying urban heat islands, assessing crop health/water stress, predicting fire risks, and modeling climate change.
Tools and Data
  • Tools : Google Earth Engine
  • Method : LST = (TIR - T_min)/(T_max - T_min)
  • Data Source : Landsat 9, Primary data extracted from Landsat 9 Imagery, USGS, processed using Google Earth Engine
Functions
  1. Land Surface Temperature Estimation: LST provides information about the temperature of the Earth's surface, which is crucial for understanding thermal dynamics in various environments.
  2. Urbanization Effects: Research often shows higher LST in built-up areas (e.g., maximum in Land Cover Zone 2) compared to vegetated areas.
