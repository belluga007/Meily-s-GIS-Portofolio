URBAN HEAT ISLAND (UHI)
Short Description
An Urban Heat Island (UHI) is a phenomenon where urban areas experience higher temperatures than surrounding rural areas due to human activities and the concentration of heat-absorbing materials like concrete and asphalt. UHI can be analyzed using remote sensing techniques, particularly by examining Land Surface Temperature (LST) data from satellites (like Landsat 8 or 9) using thermal infrared bands. This provides a "heat map" of the ground surface..
Tools and Data
  • Tools : Google Earth Engine, ArcGIS PRO
  • Method : LST Analysis, LST used to identify UHI = LST urban - LST rural
  • Data Source : Landsat 9, Primary data extracted from Landsat 9 Imagery, USGS, processed using Google Earth Engine
Functions
  1. Overlapping heat maps with socioeconomic data to see if low-income neighborhoods suffer more from heat.
  2. Simulating where planting 1,000 new trees would reduce the temperature the most.
  3. Identifying the most vulnerable areas within the city for targeted heat mitigation strategies.
